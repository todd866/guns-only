# Ejection: Shift-E, an honest seat, and the walk home

*2026-07-23. Owner: "we really need an ejection option, shift-E? … When you get it wrong and
still pancake, the worst. And then you can run around with your little man."*

- **Shift-E** commands ejection (confirm-free — hesitation is the enemy; it's already a last
  resort). Canopy, seat rail, drogue, main — as a deterministic kernel trajectory, not an
  animation.
- **The envelope is real.** A public-data ACES-class surrogate: zero-zero capable, but sink
  rate, bank, and altitude at initiation decide whether the chute opens in time. Punch out
  inverted at 100 ft in a 10,000 fpm sink and you pancake under a streaming chute — the DCS
  lesson, kept.
- **The debrief cares**: ejection decision time vs the recoverable envelope becomes a scored
  moment (tie-in: the Auto-GCAS boundary already computes exactly this).
- **The little man**: post-landing, a minimal on-foot state — walk, look, watch the fight you
  left. 2030s era renders it in the clean illustrative style; no combat, just the walk and the
  pickup. Scope: after the valley/drone-war builds.
- Kernel first (seat trajectory + envelope + outcomes + tests), presentation second, on-foot
  last.
