# The Middle Air: Flight Combat Between Study Sim and Arcade (mid-2026 survey)

### Capsule assessments (what each nails / misses for BFM)

**Tiny Combat Arena** — the closest anyone came to the brief on a flat screen, and the niche's cautionary tale. It shipped honest energy bleed ("you lose airspeed in tight turns and gain it in dives" — [Stormbirds first impressions](https://stormbirds.blog/2022/02/27/first-impressions-of-tiny-combat-arena/)), G-blackout, geometry-dependent flares, and a predictive gun pipper computing shell-target intersection over a second into the future ([Skyward FM](https://www.skywardfm.com/post/tiny-combat-arena-tca-first-impressions)). It missed: AI never got past "prototype dogfighting ability" ([0.3.0 Dogfighting Foundations devlog](https://why485.itch.io/tiny-combat-legacy/devlog/108375/030-dogfighting-foundations-update)), one flyable jet (a Harrier — a poor BFM platform), and content drought. In Feb 2026 Why485 said on Bluesky he'll never make another commercial game ([profile](https://bsky.app/profile/did:plc:vlxofaoav5oaacdtqhk5coco); widely quoted in [Steam reviews](https://steamcommunity.com/app/1347550/reviews/?browsefilter=mostrecent)). His design philosophy remains the best public articulation of the target feel: *"It's never fun dying in a game without knowing what happened… I want to give the player a very clear picture of what's going on"* ([Skyward FM interview pt 2](https://www.skywardfm.com/post/interview-why485-developer-of-tiny-combat-arena-part-2)).

**Nuclear Option** — the healthiest occupant of the gap, and proof of the demand signal: real aerodynamics + any-input-device + fictional jets (no switchology, no licensing) = 95% on ~10k reviews. But it's a missile-era *battlefield* sim, not a BFM sandbox: "If you're in gun range and don't have heatseeking missiles, something has gone very very wrong," and the AI flies with no G-limits so it "will almost always outperform you in a turning fight" ([Steam dogfight thread](https://steamcommunity.com/app/2168680/discussions/0/508449159712370124/)). Guns-only BFM is a mission-editor hack, not a mode.

**VTOL VR → Virtual Fighter Maneuvers** — the headline finding of this survey. Boundless Dynamics distilled VTOL VR into a **pure BFM game**: launch directly into the merge, ranked 1v1 dogfights (guns-only per community reporting — [Sufficient Velocity thread](https://forums.sufficientvelocity.com/threads/virtual-fighter-maneuvers-vtol-vr-devs-competitive-dogfight-sim-pcvr-meta-quest.151094/)), up to 4v4 guns+heaters, three fictional jets deliberately typed as classic BFM archetypes, realistic transonic drag and fuel-weight effects, zero switchology ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3952640/Virtual_Fighter_Maneuvers/) — verified: Boundless Dynamics, 19 Dec 2025, VR-only, 95%). **The brief's product thesis has been validated — but only in VR.** The flat-screen version of this game does not exist.

**Project Wingman / Jet Lancer / Sky Rogue** — the arcade wing. Wingman proves gun-lethality reads as a feature (gun-only runs are a community playstyle) but has no energy model and pattern-flying AI. Jet Lancer is the purest "guns and geometry" feel in 2D — and its devs did the most rigorous camera work in the niche: they instrumented beta builds and found that in 99.5% of hits taken, the projectile had been on-screen >250 ms before impact — deaths tuned to feel fair ([GameMaker showcase interview](https://gamemaker.io/en/showcase/jet-lancer), [camera discussion](https://steamcommunity.com/app/913060/discussions/0/3606765810640650629/)). Sky Rogue is loose and lock-on-dominated; its contribution is the right-stick freelook + stick-click target-lock pattern ([Steam discussion](https://steamcommunity.com/app/381020/discussions/0/1473095331492488001/)).

**CAP2** — ten years in EA, one community open letter ("a payware alpha demo" — [thread](https://steamcommunity.com/app/347170/discussions/0/4577340984555611943/)), one MicroProse-backed revival (v0.920.0, Apr 2025 — [notes](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/347170/view/503982156237767217)), then silence again. Evidence that the niche starves on execution, not demand.

## 2. The flat-screen sight problem: the solution space

Real BFM is flown with the neck — tally through the top of the canopy, lift vector on the bandit. A monitor is a 60-90° soda straw with no proprioception. Forty years of designs sort into **four families**, each trading among: keeping the bandit visible, keeping own-ship attitude awareness, and not becoming a magic sensor.

### Family 1 — Move the head (snap views → padlock → TrackIR → VR)

**Padlock** (view slaved to the target). Born in **Falcon 3.0** (1991) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falcon_3.0)); even then reviewers noted it existed because "it was all too easy to lose sight of the bandit" ([Key.Aero](https://www.key.aero/article/barons-combat-corner-part-1)). Its most engineered form is **Falcon 4.0** (1998), which shipped *three* mutually reinforcing pieces ([CombatSim article on F4 padlock views](http://www.combatsim.com/printer.php?action=review&id=194&page=1), [manual PDF](https://web.deu.edu.tr/atiksu/ana45/F4_Manual.pdf)):
- Classic padlock with a pictorial own-nose-vs-target orientation element;
- the **SA bar** — an abstract "where is my head pointed relative to my nose and the bandit" overlay;
- **EFOV** — a wide-angle cockpit-less view with inset MFD/target windows and a **rotating arrow guiding your eye to the target** (padlock + picture-in-picture + off-screen arrow in one mode). Still shipped and maintained in Falcon BMS — an EFOV arrow regression was fixed in 4.37.6 ([BMS forum](https://forum.falcon-bms.com/topic/28691/4-37-5-two-issues-3d-cockpit-and-efov)).

**Padlock's realism fault line.** IL-2 (1946 lineage) fought a famous community war over whether padlock is a cheat, resolved by giving padlock "human eyeball" rules: engages only on targets actually within view and close; **auto-drops a few seconds after the target is masked by your own airframe**; you must visually reacquire ([Mission4Today](https://www.mission4today.com/index.php?name=ForumsPro&file=viewtopic&p=158947); the debate: [SimHQ thread](http://simhq.net/forum/ubbthreads.php/topics/889737/all/IL_2_s_Great_Debate_Padlock_is.html)). In Great Battles padlock is difficulty-gated, buggy, and disabled on expert servers ([IL-2 forum](https://forum.il2sturmovik.com/topic/39292-padlock-view-on-target/)). DCS's padlock is optional and widely banned in MP as "more like a cheat" ([ED forum](https://forum.dcs.world/topic/203424-anyone-use-padlock/)).

**Why padlock is loved/hated** (the design lesson): it's the only zero-hardware way to fly continuous BFM geometry — and it (a) destroys own-ship attitude awareness (the BMS night-padlock complaint — [BMS forum](https://forum.falcon-bms.com/topic/10294/padlock-sa-help/2)), (b) disorients as the camera whips through canopy frames, (c) becomes a magic sensor unless occlusion/range-gated, (d) makes you fight the camera instead of the bandit. The SA bar and EFOV arrow exist precisely to patch (a).

**Snap views / hat-POV** — discrete check-six views; lost to head tracking because BFM needs *continuous* aspect information, not samples ([MSFS forum consensus](https://forums.flightsimulator.com/t/hat-switch-or-track-ir-for-view/651280)).

**TrackIR / head tracking** — the sim-community default. The key trick is **amplified non-linear mapping**: turn your head ~30-60° (eyes stay on screen), virtual head sweeps 180° ([TrackIR](https://www.naturalpoint.com/trackir/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TrackIR)). Now nearly free via webcam/phone AI tracking (OpenTrack, SmoothTrack, AITrack, Tobii Beam — [roundup](https://beam.eyeware.tech/3-best-eye-tracker-head-tracker-options-for-microsoft-flight-simulator/)) — arguably the decade's biggest real-world change to the sight problem. Limits: it moves the window without widening it; check-six is a cross-eyed contortion; does nothing for spotting (the bandit is still 2 pixels).

**VR** — restores actual neck-craning and stereo depth; reviewers called Star Wars: Squadrons' visual scanning workable *only* in VR ([Road to VR](https://roadtovr.com/star-wars-squadrons-review/), [Escapist](https://www.escapistmagazine.com/pick-up-your-visual-scanning-how-star-wars-squadrons-makes-vr-mode-a-necessity/)). Friction: cost, GPU load, per-eye resolution shimmer on distant contacts ([DCS VR spotting thread](https://forum.dcs.world/topic/371688-optimising-spotting-bandits-in-vr/)), fatigue. VFM's existence shows VR solves the problem completely — and fences off everyone without a headset.

### Family 2 — Move the camera off the plane

**War Thunder mouse-aim: the camera *is* the control.** The mouse steers a camera-space cursor; the "Instructor" AI flies the plane toward it within FM limits ([official wiki article](https://old-wiki.warthunder.com/Instructor/How_the_instructor_works)). Looking at the bandit and pointing at the bandit become the same act — the sight problem is *dissolved*. Cost: it defines the meta. Everyone holds perfect on-limit AoA with sniper gunnery; stick-and-rudder skill depreciates; fights collapse into energy traps and snapshots. Sim battles ban the Instructor and force full-real cockpit-only flight ([WT wiki](https://wiki.warthunder.com/gamemode/simulator_battles)).

**Ace Combat's hold-to-frame target camera** (and the console lineage): tap to cycle targets, **hold and the camera swings so the target is on screen while your own plane stays the anchor** ([AC7 Steam discussion](https://steamcommunity.com/app/502500/discussions/0/2916598577630105430/), [Push Square guide](https://www.pushsquare.com/news/2019/02/guide_ace_combat_7_skies_unknown_-_tips_and_tricks_for_beginners)). Veterans hold it near-continuously; the failure mode is control-frame confusion when stick inputs no longer match the camera frame ([GameFAQs: looping while locked](https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/boards/184015-ace-combat-7-skies-unknown/77447442)). Same pattern in Crimson Skies ([controls](https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/xbox/559078-crimson-skies-high-road-to-revenge/faqs/49634)), Sky Rogue, Project Wingman. The Falconeer shows the failure case — full lock "at the cost of losing… any orientation of how you're flying" drew review complaints ([Gamecritics](https://gamecritics.com/gc-staff/the-falconeer-review/)).

**The arcade convergence, stated plainly:** keep the *own ship* anchored on screen and orbit the camera toward the bandit, letting the ship — not the head — carry the sense of "which way is up." This is the exact inverse of cockpit padlock, and it's why it doesn't disorient.

**Dual-framing cameras.** Jet Lancer's dynamic pan/zoom is the best-documented indie implementation (with the 99.5%-visibility fairness telemetry — [GameMaker interview](https://gamemaker.io/en/showcase/jet-lancer)). The general toolbox (weighted multi-target framing, smoothing, dynamic zoom) is Squirrel Eiserloh's GDC talk "Juicing Your Cameras With Math" ([GDC Vault](https://gdcvault.com/play/1023146/Math-for-Game-Programmers-Juicing)). No GDC talk specifically on two-ship dogfight framing was found; academic edge is multi-focus framing/dynamic split-screen ("Voron_eye," 2023 — [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/370285558_Voron_eye_A_deep_dive_into_video_game_camera_framing_with_multiple_points_of_focus_and_the_exploration_of_the_dynamic_split-screen)).

**Tiny Combat Arena's hybrid**: in mouse mode, an orbit camera decoupled from the nose where **target selection follows the camera's gaze, not the nose** ([thread](https://steamcommunity.com/app/1347550/discussions/0/3414305314932218664/)) — plus F5 padlock, markers, and TrackIR/Tobii simultaneously. Why485's public camera-design trail is worth reading end-to-end: padlock with HUD cues and fixing the "sudden snap when cancelling padlock" ([his KSP-forum posts](https://forum.kerbalspaceprogram.com/profile/26795-why485/content/?type=forums_topic_post)); rebuilding flight-sim cameras in Cinemachine and rejecting it ([tumblr](https://why485.tumblr.com/post/190886980612/been-messing-with-cinemachine-to-see-how-well-it)); adding formation-flying sway to external cams so the chase cam feels like "another ship in formation filming the shot" ([tumblr](https://why485.tumblr.com/post/180722372668/thanks-to-you-i-made-a-flying-helicopter-now-i)). (Note: `why485.tumblr.com` 403s automated fetches; use `www.tumblr.com/why485/<post-id>`.)

### Family 3 — Replace sight with symbols

- **Off-screen edge arrows** — the generic pattern ([tutorial](https://code.tutsplus.com/positioning-on-screen-indicators-to-point-to-off-screen-targets--gamedev-6644t)); Falcon 4's EFOV arrow (1998) is the ancestor; Jet Lancer's HUD range/direction indicators the 2D version.
- **Through-airframe bandit diamonds / labels** — IL-2 icons, DCS labels; difficulty-gated, banned full-real.
- **SA instruments** — Falcon 4's SA bar remains the canonical "where is my head" display.
- **Auto target markers within a detection radius** — Tiny Combat Arena's "your eyes as a sensor" abstraction; marker clutter was debated ([UI thread](https://steamcommunity.com/app/1347550/discussions/0/3843304884863510968/)).
- **Spotting-aid rendering** (adjacent, essential): Falcon BMS "Smart Scaling" draws distant aircraft larger-than-life so detection ranges match human-eye reality; Why485 built an interactive demo of these techniques ([Smart Scaling Demonstration](https://why485.itch.io/smart-scaling-demonstration)) and analyzed War Thunder's visibility system ([tumblr](https://why485.tumblr.com/post/130832704978/war-thunder-development-visibility-in-153)).
- **Audio as padlock substitute** — Heatblur's Jester AI (DCS F-14) calls bandit position through the merge with 4,000+ lines ([Heatblur blog](https://store.heatblur.com/blogs/news/f-14-v2-8-jamming-jester-and-headless-bodies)); an AI backseater/wingman calling tally is an underused flat-screen solution.
- **Picture-in-picture bandit window** — TIE Fighter's in-fiction Combat Multi-view Display (1994) ([Wookieepedia](https://starwars.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Multi-view_Display)); BMS's EFOV inset. **No 2020s indie ships one — open design space.**

### Family 4 — Change the projection (least explored)

FOV sliders are universal; beyond that, Panini/cylindrical projections can render 150°+ convincingly ([Pannini writeup](http://tksharpless.net/vedutismo/Pannini/)), and shaunlebron's Quake "blinky" demo explored peripheral rendering ([GitHub](https://github.com/shaunlebron/blinky)) — but **no shipped flight sim uses non-rectilinear projection as a dogfight solution**. Another open lane, with known readability risks.

## 3. Engine choice for a solo hobbyist dogfight game

**Godot 4.x — current state (verified):** latest stable is **4.7.1 (14 Jul 2026)**; 4.7 landed 18 Jun 2026 ([official archive](https://godotengine.org/download/archive/)). Jolt Physics was merged into core in 4.4 and became the default for new 3D projects in 4.6 ([4.6 release page](https://godotengine.org/releases/4.6/)).

**Flight model:** [**godot_aerodynamic_physics** by addmix](https://github.com/addmix/godot_aerodynamic_physics) (verified: MIT, 193 stars, v0.9.1 released 5 Jun 2026, actively maintained, C++ port in progress) — `AeroBody3D` + per-surface `AeroSurface3D` nodes, roadmap includes energy-manoeuvrability profiling. Also [fbcosentino's simplified flightsim](https://github.com/fbcosentino/godot-simplified-flightsim) (game-oriented, combined coefficients). No maintained JSBSim port — and for a dogfight *game* JSBSim is overkill; TCA and Project Wingman both shipped custom simplified models ([Why485 interview](https://www.skywardfm.com/post/interview-why485-developer-of-tiny-combat-arena-part-2)).

**Large world:** single precision is fine to ~8 km from origin ([Large World Coordinates docs](https://docs.godotengine.org/en/stable/tutorials/physics/large_world_coordinates.html)) — a jet at 300 m/s crosses that in 30 s, so you must handle it. Double-precision builds exist but require compiling the engine + templates yourself, and shaders stay single-precision (camera-relative emulation: [engine blog](https://godotengine.org/article/emulating-double-precision-gpu-render-large-worlds/)). Known jitter issues far from origin: [#58516](https://github.com/godotengine/godot/issues/58516), [#75537](https://github.com/godotengine/godot/issues/75537). **Practical call: skip double precision; use standard builds + origin shifting for a ~50 km combat bubble** (reference implementation write-up: [Frozen Fractal](https://frozenfractal.com/blog/2024/4/11/around-the-world-14-floating-the-origin/); [official demo](https://github.com/godotengine/godot-demo-projects/blob/master/misc/large_world_coordinates/README.md)).

**Terrain:** [Terrain3D (TokisanGames)](https://github.com/TokisanGames/Terrain3D) — healthy, clipmap-based, up to **65.5×65.5 km at full resolution** (~100 km with vertex spacing) ([docs](https://terrain3d.readthedocs.io/en/stable/docs/double_precision.html)). Clipmap LOD is inherently view-distance-friendly, but **no documented flight-altitude case study exists** — prototype "looks good from 20,000 ft with a 60 km horizon" in week one. Occlusion culling is useless for open sky (docs say so — [occlusion culling](https://docs.godotengine.org/en/stable/tutorials/3d/occlusion_culling.html)); use mesh LOD + visibility ranges. Long-distance directional shadows are a known weak spot ([proposal #5464](https://github.com/godotengine/godot-proposals/issues/5464)) — which matters little at altitude.

**Existence proofs:** no commercially credible *jet* combat game has shipped in Godot 4 — but [RotorSim](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3376070/RotorSim_Helicopter_Simulator/) (solo-dev sim-cade helicopter sim, 1.0 Jul 2025, dev went full-time) and Road to Vostok (solo, big outdoor world, ~140k first-week sales — [GamingOnLinux](https://www.gamingonlinux.com/2026/04/road-to-vostok-is-an-incredibly-impressive-solo-developed-hardcore-survival-shooter/)) prove the solo-3D-shipping path. Open-source references: [ZenithFlightSim](https://github.com/exocetfalling/ZenithFlightSim), [gd-flight](https://github.com/andrew-wilkes/gd-flight), [KidsCanCode airplane recipe](https://kidscancode.org/godot_recipes/4.x/3d/simple_airplane/index.html).

**Alternatives, honestly weighed:**

| Engine | Verdict for this project |
|---|---|
| **Godot 4.7** | Best "actually finish it" odds: fastest iteration, MIT, Jolt default, maintained aero addon, Terrain3D at flight scale. You hand-roll origin shifting; thinnest flight-genre track record. |
| **Unity 6** | The modern indie air-combat canon *is* Unity (Nuclear Option, TCA, VTOL VR); only real flight-model asset market ([Silantro](https://assetstore.unity.com/packages/tools/physics/silantro-flight-simulator-toolkit-128025)); most training data → best LLM codegen. Runtime Fee cancelled Sep 2024 ([Unity](https://unity.com/blog/unity-is-canceling-the-runtime-fee)), trust scarred. Materially better if you want to *buy* your flight model. |
| **Unreal 5** | Built-in large-world coordinates + World Partition + official JSBSim plugin ([repo](https://github.com/JSBSim-Team/jsbsim/tree/master/UnrealEngine)); Project Wingman proves the genre ships in it — in ~5 years. Heavyweight for solo/small-scope. |
| **Bevy** | Not recommended: no stable editor, breaking changes every release, no committed 1.0 ([bevy.org/news](https://bevy.org/news/)). |
| **three.js/web** | Best AI-assistance and distribution story (fly.pieter.com was vibe-coded in hours — [404 Media's corrective](https://www.404media.co/this-game-created-by-ai-vibe-coding-makes-50-000-a-month-yours-probably-wont/)); lowest terrain/fidelity ceiling. Viable if "playable in a browser tab" is the goal. |

**Bottom line:** Godot is defensible and current; Unity is the genre-proven alternative with a purchasable flight model; nothing else is materially better for this scope. Nobody has shipped a credible jet-combat game in Godot 4 — no proven path, open lane.

## 4. What makes BFM feel right — design insights and sources

### Energy honesty is the contract
The single most consistent finding across a decade of DCS/BMS forum archaeology: **experienced players detect energy cheating specifically**, and it is the number-one immersion killer. DCS AI "sucks every inch of performance from the aircraft, and it even goes beyond what is physically possible" ([ED forum](https://forum.dcs.world/topic/149315-ai-cheating-behavior-taking-all-the-fun-out/), [2025 thread](https://forum.dcs.world/topic/374780-is-ai-cheating-or-am-i-just-bad-at-dogfighting-or-both/)); Nuclear Option's no-G-limit AI draws the same complaint. Falcon BMS dev candor on the balance dilemma: "Make AI too good and you'll lose people… make it too easy and we'll be bashed like DCS" ([BMS forum](https://forum.falcon-bms.com/post/453947)). **Design rule: AI flies the same flight model with the same bleed rates; difficulty = decision quality + gunnery error, never thrust/G cheats** (TCA's Rookie/Veteran/Ace tiers express difficulty as *behavior* — [devlog](https://why485.itch.io/tiny-combat-legacy/devlog/108375/030-dogfighting-foundations-update)).

### Feel is authored on top of the model
- **Why485's corpus** (the essential indie reading): he tried ~5 arcade FMs and rejected the standard velocity-toward-nose hack because it *feels* wrong before it's wrong physically ([tumblr Q&A](https://www.tumblr.com/why485/172608146783)); a more stable, energy-honest FM made "the gun much easier to use," shifting skill "onto the flying than the gunnery" — while he candidly worried "a realistic configuration isn't very fun to fly" ([flight model demo essay](https://why485.itch.io/tiny-combat-arena-flight-model-demo), [flight model test](https://why485.itch.io/tiny-combat-legacy-flight-model-test)). His cheap, legible energy tells: control stiffening with airspeed, over-wing vapor at critical AoA, per-aircraft corner speeds.
- **G-effects, with liftable numbers**: IL-2's physiology dev blog is the best public spec — at +7-8G: greyout 3.5 s, tunnel 5.3 s, blackout 7.3 s, G-LOC 8.9 s; push-pull effect; G warm-up; AGSM ([Dev Blog #263](https://il2sturmovik.com/news/582/dev-blog-263/)). DCS's re-tune shows even hardcore sims stretch G-effects for playability ([2.9.4 changelog](https://www.digitalcombatsimulator.com/en/news/changelog/release/2.9.4.53549/)).
- **Stylization beats data**: AC7's sound director — "It didn't sound like ACE COMBAT at all when we input the real data… the exciting, dramatic feeling was lost" ([Bandai Namco making-of](https://www.bandainamcostudios.com/en/behind-the-game/205)). Project Wingman's testers *demanded* pre-stall buffet and shake because stalls felt "empty" ([itch devlog/comments](https://rb-d2.itch.io/wingman/devlog)).
- **Speed sensation** is relative to nearby objects — altitude kills it; FOV widening with speed is the cheapest effective trick ([Couvignou](https://elliotdev.gg/adding-the-feeling-of-speed/)).

### The gunsight is the pursuit-geometry tutorial
- **Andy Bush's SimHQ Air Combat Corner** (former USAF F-4/AT-38 pilot writing BFM/gunnery for sim players): putting the LCOS pipper on the bandit *is* flying lead pursuit — the sight is pursuit geometry made visible ([library index](https://www.simhq.net/articles-library/air-combat-corner-library.html), [Lag Pursuit](https://www.simhq.net/_air/air_017a.html), [Pure and Lead Pursuit](https://www.simhq.net/_air/air_018a.html)). (Site 403s bots; loads in a browser.)
- **The gyro gunsight is a ready-made skill mechanic**: gyro supplies lead, *pilot* supplies range by twisting a knob to fit the reticle to the target's wingspan ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyro_gunsight), [USNI on the Mk 14](https://www.usni.org/magazines/naval-history/2025/february/inside-mk-14-gyroscopic-gunsight)). LCOS math in implementable form: ["A Digital Lead Computing Optical Sight Model"](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/235021202_A_Digital_Lead_Computing_Optical_Sight_Model).
- **The cautionary tale is War Thunder's lead indicator** — always-correct-for-current-velocity, hotly debated as a de-skiller ([forum](https://forum.warthunder.com/t/new-arcade-aiming/215998)). An honest-but-imperfect sight (LCOS lag-and-settle, or gyro + manual ranging) keeps gun kills earned.
- Per-part damage (IL-2 [4.005 DM overhaul](https://il2sturmovik.com/news/540/update-4005/)) is what makes a short tracking burst feel consequential vs hit-point sponges.

### AI adversary architecture
- **Doctrine sources**: Robert Shaw, *Fighter Combat: Tactics and Maneuvering* — the canon ([USNI](https://www.usni.org/press/books/fighter-combat), [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/fightercombattac0000shaw)); **CNATRA P-825**, the US Navy's actual student BFM curriculum (Rev 07-14, 410 pp), free and public, including the *perceptual cues* students use to assess the other jet (official cnatra.navy.mil URL is dead as of Jul 2026 — verified mirror: [falcon.blu3wolf.com/Docs/P-825.pdf](http://falcon.blu3wolf.com/Docs/P-825.pdf)); Kylander's *In Pursuit* — BFM written specifically for fighting inside a computer screen's constraints ([PDF](http://www.virtualpilots.fi/feature/lento_ohjeet/inpursuit/inpursuit.pdf)).
- **The most game-practical AI blueprint remains NASA's Adaptive Maneuvering Logic** (1970s-80s): each decision tick, generate candidate elementary maneuvers, forward-simulate briefly, score with an evaluation function over relative geometry/energy, execute the best ([NASA CR-4160, 1988](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/api/citations/19890018022/downloads/19890018022.pdf)). Modern validation that "write the BFM manual as code" works: [AIAA "Manual-Based Automated Maneuvering Decisions"](https://arc.aiaa.org/doi/full/10.2514/1.I011234). Indie practicality note: behavior trees thrash on rapid state transitions — use fatter maneuver nodes ([Space Battle Arcade devlog](https://www.gamedev.net/blogs/entry/2272802-devblog-10-making-dogfight-ai-behavior-trees/)).
- **RL is a warning, not a recipe**: DARPA AlphaDogfight's champion beat an F-16 Weapons School grad 5-0 with superhuman frame-perfect forward-quarter gun snapshots and no self-preservation ([DARPA](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2020/alphadogfight-trial), [Breaking Defense](https://breakingdefense.com/2020/08/ai-slays-top-f-16-pilot-in-darpa-dogfight-simulation/)) — exactly the behaviors that make a game opponent feel cheap. If you want the literature anyway: [hierarchical RL (Lockheed, 2nd place ADT)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.00990), [2024 survey + tutorial](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-023-10620-2).
- **Minimal credible-AI feature list** from Nuclear Option's patch notes: avoids threats in flight path, RTBs low-fuel, weighs probability of success with remaining weapons, leads fast targets imperfectly ([wiki/dev notes](https://nuclearoption.wiki.gg/wiki/Development)).

### Fighter pilots on games
- **C.W. "Mover" Lemoine** (F-16/F-18) — most prolific real-pilot evaluator of sim BFM ([channel](https://www.youtube.com/@CWLemoine)); recurring theme: sims nail procedures and geometry, miss the *physiological cost* (G strain, seat-of-pants energy cues).
- **Growling Sidewinder's guest-pilot fights** (e.g., [vs a Rafale pilot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zgDZ4BLk0Pw)) — a skilled gamer repeatedly pressuring real fighter pilots in DCS is itself a data point: screen BFM rewards HOTAS dexterity and consequence-free G, so real-world excellence only partially transfers.
- **Fighter Pilot Podcast Ep. 028** — Vincent "Jell-O" Aiello (TOPGUN instructor) at Eagle Dynamics, bowled over by VR ([discussion](https://www.pilotsofamerica.com/community/threads/air-combat-simulation-gaming.114951/)).
- One-circle/two-circle, rate vs radius in accessible written form: [The Aviationist BFM explainer](https://theaviationist.com/2020/06/07/here-are-the-basic-fighter-maneuvers-fundamentals-you-need-to-understand-dogfights-explained-by-a-fighter-pilot/).

## 5. Synthesis: the gap, precisely stated

1. **The product thesis is validated but unclaimed on flat screens.** Guns-only ranked BFM with a real energy model and zero switchology exists, is loved (95%), and is called Virtual Fighter Maneuvers — but it is VR-only. Nuclear Option (95%, ~10k reviews) proves "real physics, any input device, fictional jets" sells, but it's a missile battlefield. Tiny Combat Arena proved the *feel* on a flat screen and died of solo-dev economics, not of the idea. Nobody has shipped: **real energy model + guns-first BFM + a first-class flat-screen camera answer.**
2. **The camera answer can be assembled from proven parts**: arcade-style own-ship-anchored orbit-to-target camera (the convergent solution that doesn't disorient) + IL-2-style "human eyeball" padlock rules for fairness + a Falcon-4-SA-bar-descendant attitude cue + gaze-follows-camera target selection (TCA) + optional webcam head tracking (now free). The unexplored lanes are the PiP bandit window and non-rectilinear projection.
3. **The AI is the differentiator, not the flight model.** Every game in the niche has weak or cheating AI, and players detect energy cheating specifically. NASA AML (trial maneuvers + evaluation function) with a Shaw/P-825 maneuver library, flying the *player's* flight model, with difficulty as decision quality and gunnery error, would exceed the field.
4. **Timing**: AC8 (2 Oct 2026) and Korea. IL-2 (Steam 4 Aug 2026) will pull mainstream attention to air combat within months, and Korea's explicit accessibility push (gamepads, no start-up sequences) shows the majors converging on the middle space from above.
