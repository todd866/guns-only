# Guns Only persistent world contract

The global room is a bounded presence service and the seed of a future authoritative combat
world. It is deliberately explicit about what it does **not** yet authorize.

## Authority boundary

- Stable `pilotKey` values map a browser installation to a persistent callsign and 40 km-spaced
  spawn sector. They are pseudonymous browser credentials, not authenticated player accounts.
- Player poses, mission/presentation IDs, entity IDs, lifecycle, and impact evidence are
  client-reported. Snapshots stamp them `authority: "client-presence"` and
  `combatEligible: false`; combat code must never treat them as hit, damage, or scoring truth.
- World bogey motion is generated by the Durable Object and stamped `authority: "server-world"`,
  but bogeys remain `combatEligible: false` until weapons, damage, and lifecycle all execute in
  the authoritative world.
- Inland clients apply the inverse assigned `spawnOrigin` to both the simulation terrain provider
  and its rendered Korea substrate. A local pose plus its origin therefore samples the same hill
  and produces the same AGL/impact surface that another observer sees at that world coordinate.
- Carrier qualification and maintenance sorties are still local training instances: each owns a
  carrier at local zero and an offshore terrain placement. Their contacts are intentionally not
  rendered in the shared inland frame until the world service has authoritative carrier-instance
  identity and handoff; the client never silently composites the two coordinate systems.
- `alive` means combat-capable. `bodyPresent` independently keeps a terminal aircraft visible.
  `terminalState` and `impactSurface` teach peers whether that body is flying, falling, impacted,
  settled, water/deck/structure affected, or retained at the simulation boundary.
- `streamId` is assigned per connection. A changed stream or per-sortie `entityId` is a hard
  interpolation discontinuity, so reloads and restarts cannot smear an aircraft across the map.

## Transport and lifecycle

- Protocol v2 accepts one bounded JSON hello followed by monotonically increasing pose messages.
- ACTIVE state publishes at 20 Hz. READY, PAUSED, and FINISHED publish at 1 Hz, with lifecycle,
  entity, physical-presence, and impact transitions sent immediately.
- The worker accepts at most 64 online pilots plus eight pending handshakes. A token bucket permits
  brief network bursts but rejects sustained input above the 20 Hz contract.
- Handshakes time out after five seconds. A connection with no valid pose for 15 seconds is closed;
  the browser clears remote contacts immediately on disconnect, world-epoch change, or stop.
- A new connection for the same persistent pilot deterministically replaces the older socket.
- Origins are exact configured origins. `localhost` is not implicitly trusted at arbitrary ports.

## Scale boundary

Each recipient sees at most 64 nearby pilots and 16 nearby sectors (48 bogeys) inside a 120 km
interest radius. That bounds each payload, but one global Durable Object still performs recipient
fan-out. The 64-connection ceiling is therefore a conservative safety limit, not a measured scale
claim.

The next scale step is sector/interest Durable Object sharding with explicit handoff between
adjacent sectors, followed by load tests that measure messages, CPU, egress, reconnect storms, and
alarm cost. Raising the ceiling before that work would hide an architectural limit rather than
solve it.
